# Empirical

## Definition
Empirical refers to knowledge or methods derived from observation, experience, or experiment, as opposed to theory or pure reasoning. It denotes the approach of acquiring knowledge through systematic observation and measurement of phenomena.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Ground knowledge in observable and measurable evidence
- **Key aspects**:
  - Observation (systematic watching and recording)
  - Experimentation (controlled testing of hypotheses)
  - Data collection (quantitative and qualitative measurement)
- **Types**:
  - Empirical evidence (data from observation/experiment)
  - Empirical method (scientific inquiry process)
  - Empirical knowledge (knowledge grounded in experience)
- **Role**: Foundation of empirical sciences, distinguishing experiential knowledge from a priori or speculative knowledge

## Context
In empirical sciences, the empirical aspect requires that hypotheses be testable and conclusions be grounded in observable phenomena. This distinguishes empirical inquiry from purely theoretical or mathematical approaches, though the two are often complementary.
