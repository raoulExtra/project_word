# Physical

## Definition
Physical refers to the quality or state of being related to matter, energy, space, time, and the fundamental constituents of the natural world, as studied through the natural sciences, particularly physics.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Describe properties and phenomena governed by physical laws
- **Key aspects**:
  - Matter and energy (mass-energy equivalence)
  - Space and time (spacetime structure)
  - Causation (physical causation chains)
  - Measurement (quantitative physical properties)
- **Types**:
  - Classical physical (Newtonian mechanics, Maxwell's equations)
  - Quantum physical (subatomic, probabilistic phenomena)
  - Relativistic physical (Einstein's relativity)
  - Emergent physical (complex system behaviors)
- **Role**: Provides the framework for understanding the material world and its fundamental laws

## Context
In physics, physical aspects distinguish phenomena that follow natural laws from abstract or virtual constructs. Physical properties like mass, charge, and spin are fundamental to particle physics, while physical processes like motion, decay, and interaction form the basis of all observable phenomena.
