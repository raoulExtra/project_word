# Cosmic

## Definition
Cosmic refers to everything that exists or can exist in the universe, including all matter, energy, space, time, and the fundamental physical processes that govern reality at the largest scales and earliest times.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Understand the totality of existence and the fundamental laws governing all phenomena
- **Key aspects**:
  - Cosmic structure (large-scale organization of universe)
  - Cosmic evolution (development from initial conditions to present)
  - Cosmic composition (matter, energy, dark components)
  - Cosmic principles (fundamental physical laws)
- **Types**:
  - Observable cosmos (accessible through telescopes and instruments)
  - Theoretical cosmos (conceptual models of reality)
  - Local cosmos (nearest stellar systems and galaxies)
  - Universal cosmos (entire expanse of existence)
- **Role**: Provides the ultimate context for all existence, establishing the physical and spatial framework within which all history, life, and consciousness unfold

## Context
Cosmic perspective reveals that all existence - from subatomic particles to galaxies, from single-celled life to human consciousness - occurs within the same cosmic framework governed by the same fundamental laws. Understanding the cosmic scale illuminates the profound interconnectedness of all phenomena and the deep time processes that shaped everything we observe.
