# Boundary

## Definition
A boundary is a limit, edge, or constraint that defines the scope, extent, or limits of a model, system, or analysis, demarcating what is included versus excluded from consideration.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Define the limits of analysis to focus on relevant aspects
- **Key aspects**:
  - Spatial (geographic or physical limits)
  - Temporal (time period covered)
  - Conceptual (domain of discourse)
  - Scope (level of detail)
- **Types**:
  - Explicit boundaries (clearly defined limits)
  - Implicit boundaries (assumed limits)
  - Dynamic boundaries (change over time)
  - System boundaries (environment separation)
- **Role**: Essential for defining what a model or system encompasses

## Context
Boundaries are critical for model design and interpretation. Clear boundaries prevent scope creep and ensure that analysis remains focused on relevant factors.

**Related terms**: model, assumption, scope
