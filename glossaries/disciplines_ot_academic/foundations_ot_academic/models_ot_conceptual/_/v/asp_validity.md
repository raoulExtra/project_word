# Validity

## Definition
Validity refers to the extent to which a model correctly represents the reality it purports to model, ensuring that the model's conclusions and predictions are sound and reliable.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Ensure model produces meaningful and accurate results
- **Key aspects**:
  - Construct validity (correctly represents concepts)
  - Internal validity (correct causal relationships)
  - External validity (generalizable beyond sample)
  - Face validity (appears correct to experts)
- **Types**:
  - Statistical validity (mathematical correctness)
  - Ecological validity (real-world applicability)
  - Theoretical validity (alignment with theory)
- **Role**: Fundamental measure of model quality and trustworthiness

## Context
Validity is the cornerstone of credible modeling. Without validity, a model cannot reliably inform decisions or understanding. Validity assessment is integral to model development and evaluation.

**Related terms**: model, fidelity, accuracy
