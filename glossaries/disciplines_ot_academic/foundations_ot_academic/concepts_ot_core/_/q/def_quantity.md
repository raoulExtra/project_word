# Quantity

## Definition
A quantity is a property of a phenomenon, body, or system that can be measured or expressed as a numerical value, typically in relation to a unit of measurement.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Express measurable characteristics in standardized numerical terms
- **Key aspects**:
  - **Magnitude**: The numerical size or extent
  - **Unit**: Standardized reference for measurement
  - **Dimension**: Fundamental category of measurement
- **Types**:
  - **Extensive quantities**: Depend on amount of matter (mass, volume)
  - **Intensive quantities**: Independent of amount (temperature, density)
  - **Discrete quantities**: Countable whole numbers
  - **Continuous quantities**: Infinitely divisible values
- **Role**: Fundamental to scientific measurement, mathematical modeling, and knowledge representation

## Context
In core concepts, quantity is foundational to all empirical sciences and mathematical reasoning. It provides the bridge between observed phenomena and quantitative analysis, enabling precise description and comparison.
