# Complexity

## Definition
Complexity refers to the degree to which a system, concept, or structure exhibits intricate relationships, emergent properties, and non-linear behaviors that arise from the interactions of its components.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Understand and model systems with intricate interconnections
- **Key aspects**:
  - **Emergence**: Properties that arise from component interactions
  - **Non-linearity**: Small changes can have large effects
  - **Self-organization**: Order arising from local interactions
  - **Adaptive behavior**: Response to environmental changes
- **Types**:
  - Simple complexity (few components, limited interactions)
  - Complex complexity (many components, rich interactions)
  - Dynamic complexity (changing over time)
  - Static complexity (fixed structure)
- **Role**: Enables modeling and analysis of systems beyond simple aggregation

## Context
In core concepts, complexity is fundamental to understanding how systems behave in ways that cannot be predicted from their individual parts. It bridges simple systems and real-world phenomena, making it essential for ontology engineering and knowledge representation.
