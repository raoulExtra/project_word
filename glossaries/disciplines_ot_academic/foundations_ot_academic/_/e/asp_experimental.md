# Experimental

## Definition
Experimental refers to the methodological approach of testing hypotheses, theories, or models through controlled observation, manipulation, and measurement in natural or artificial environments to validate, refute, or refine scientific understanding.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Verify scientific claims and discover new phenomena through systematic testing
- **Key aspects**:
  - Hypothesis testing (controlled experiments)
  - Variable manipulation (independent, dependent, control)
  - Replication (reproducibility of results)
- **Types**:
  - Laboratory experiments (controlled environments)
  - Field experiments (natural settings)
  - Observational studies (without manipulation)
  - Quasi-experimental designs (limited control)
- **Role**: Fundamental to empirical sciences, enabling the transition from theory to validated knowledge

## Context
In core concepts, experimental methodology provides the bridge between theoretical predictions and empirical verification. It is central to the scientific method across physics, biology, psychology, medicine, and social sciences, forming the basis for evidence-based knowledge construction.
