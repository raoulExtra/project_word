# Critical

## Definition
Critical reasoning is the cognitive process of analyzing, evaluating, and synthesizing information to form judgments, identify assumptions, and assess the validity and reliability of arguments, concepts, and beliefs.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Evaluate and improve reasoning by examining underlying assumptions and logical structures
- **Key aspects**:
  - Analysis (breaking down complex ideas into component parts)
  - Evaluation (assessing the strength and validity of arguments)
  - Synthesis (integrating insights into coherent understanding)
- **Types**:
  - Critical thinking (systematic evaluation of knowledge)
  - Critical reasoning (logical assessment of arguments)
  - Critical theory (evaluating social and political structures)
- **Role**: Enables discernment, intellectual rigor, and the identification of bias, fallacies, and unstated assumptions

## Context
In philosophy, critical reasoning is fundamental to epistemology, logic, and ethics. It distinguishes thoughtful reflection from uncritical acceptance, enabling individuals to make informed judgments and contribute to rational discourse. Critical reasoning is essential in academia, professional practice, and civic engagement.
