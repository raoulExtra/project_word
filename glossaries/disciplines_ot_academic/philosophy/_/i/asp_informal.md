# Informal

## Definition
Informal reasoning refers to the use of logical principles and argumentative structures outside of formal logical systems, encompassing everyday reasoning, natural language argumentation, and practical decision-making in real-world contexts.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Apply logical reasoning to natural language arguments and everyday situations
- **Key aspects**:
  - Natural language argumentation (reasoning in spoken/written discourse)
  - Fallacy identification (recognizing errors in reasoning)
  - Context-dependent reasoning (considering situational factors)
- **Types**:
  - Persuasive reasoning (convincing audiences)
  - Practical reasoning (decision-making in complex situations)
  - Rhetorical reasoning (argumentation in literature and debate)
- **Role**: Bridges formal logic with human communication and practical decision-making

## Context
In philosophy, informal reasoning is studied in logic, rhetoric, and epistemology. Unlike formal reasoning which operates within defined logical systems, informal reasoning must handle ambiguity, context, and the complexities of natural language. It is essential for critical thinking, legal argumentation, and effective communication.
