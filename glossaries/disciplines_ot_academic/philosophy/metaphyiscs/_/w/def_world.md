# World

## Definition
A world is a complete, coherent structure or reality that encompasses all of existence, including its fundamental constituents, laws, and relations. Worlds may be actual (existing) or possible (logically consistent alternatives).

## Details
- **Purpose**: Provide a comprehensive framework for understanding complete states of affairs
- **Key aspects**:
  - Actual world (this world, spatiotemporal continuum)
  - Possible worlds (logically consistent alternatives)
  - Modal semantics (world-relative truth)
- **Types**:
  - Physical world (governed by natural laws)
  - Metaphysical world (fundamental ontological structure)
  - Conceptual world (mental or abstract constructions)
- **Role**: Central to modal logic, metaphysical analysis, and philosophical semantics

## Context
In metaphysics, the concept of world is foundational to understanding possibility, necessity, identity, and the structure of reality. Possible world semantics is a key tool in modal logic, while the nature of the actual world remains a central metaphysical question.
