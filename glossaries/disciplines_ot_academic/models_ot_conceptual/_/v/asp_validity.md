# Validity

## Definition
Validity is the quality of a model, theory, or measurement being correct, accurate, and appropriate for the purpose it is intended to serve, representing the extent to which it truly captures the phenomenon it claims to describe.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Ensure models accurately represent reality
- **Key aspects**:
  - Construct validity (correct concepts)
  - External validity (generalizability)
  - Internal validity (causal relationships)
- **Assessment**:
  - Comparison with empirical data
  - Peer review and critique
  - Replication studies

## Context
Validity is the cornerstone of scientific rigor, determining whether a model can be trusted for its intended purpose and used for decision-making or further research.
