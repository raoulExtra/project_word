# Assumption

## Definition
An assumption is a foundational premise or accepted truth that serves as a basis for reasoning, model construction, or theoretical development, often representing what is taken to be true without proof.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Enable modeling by accepting certain conditions as given
- **Key aspects**:
  - Simplification of complex reality
  - Explicit acknowledgment of limitations
  - Basis for logical derivation
- **Types**:
  - Explicit assumptions (clearly stated)
  - Implicit assumptions (unstated but implied)
  - Working assumptions (provisional acceptance)

## Context
Assumptions are essential in model construction, allowing modelers to focus on relevant aspects while acknowledging that models are simplifications of reality.
