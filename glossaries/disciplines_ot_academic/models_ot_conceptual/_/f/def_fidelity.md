# Fidelity

## Definition
Fidelity is the degree to which a model accurately represents the essential characteristics and behaviors of the system or phenomenon it models, indicating the quality and faithfulness of the representation.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Measure how well a model captures reality
- **Key aspects**:
  - Structural fidelity (correct relationships)
  - Behavioral fidelity (correct dynamics)
  - Visual fidelity (appearance accuracy)
- **Trade-offs**: Higher fidelity often requires more complexity and computational resources

## Context
Fidelity is a critical measure in model evaluation, guiding decisions about model selection and refinement for specific applications.
