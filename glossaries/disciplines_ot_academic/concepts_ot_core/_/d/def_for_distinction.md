# Distinction

## Definition
A distinction is the act of identifying differences between concepts, objects, or ideas, serving as a fundamental cognitive operation for classification and understanding.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Separate, differentiate, or identify unique characteristics
- **Key aspects**:
  - Boundary definition
  - Category separation
  - Identity recognition
- **Types**:
  - Essential distinction (fundamental differences)
  - Accidental distinction (superficial differences)
  - Contextual distinction (differences based on situation)
- **Role**: Enables cognition, categorization, and logical reasoning

## Context
Distinction is foundational to all knowledge work, allowing us to separate concepts, define boundaries, and create meaningful classifications across all domains of study.
