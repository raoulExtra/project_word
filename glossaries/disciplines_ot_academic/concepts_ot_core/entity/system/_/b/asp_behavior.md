# Behavior

## Definition
Behavior refers to the actions, responses, or reactions of an entity or system in response to internal states, external stimuli, or interactions with other entities. It describes the dynamic characteristics that distinguish how a system operates over time.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Describe and model how entities or systems respond to conditions and inputs
- **Key aspects**:
  - Observable actions (detectable outputs or changes)
  - Responsive reactions (outputs triggered by inputs or stimuli)
  - Temporal patterns (recurring or evolving behavior sequences)
  - Deterministic vs. non-deterministic behavior
- **Types**:
  - Predictable behavior (following known rules or patterns)
  - Adaptive behavior (changing responses based on experience or context)
  - Emergent behavior (arising from component interactions)
  - Anomalous behavior (deviations from expected patterns)
- **Role**: Enables understanding of system dynamics, supports prediction, control, and verification of system performance

## Context
In core concepts, behavior is fundamental to understanding how entities and systems operate. While structure describes what a system is composed of and state describes its condition, behavior describes what a system does. Behavior analysis is essential for ontology engineering, systems modeling, and understanding how entities respond to their environment.
