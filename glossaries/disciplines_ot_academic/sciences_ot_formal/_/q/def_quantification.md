# Quantification

## Definition
Quantification is the process of assigning a quantity or number to elements within a domain, typically expressed through logical operators that determine how many objects satisfy a given property. For detailed treatment in formal logic, see [quantification in logic](sciences_ot_formal/mathematics/logic/_/q/def_quantification_in_logic.md).

## Details
- **Purpose**: Express scope and extent in formal systems
- **Key aspects**:
  - Universal quantification (all elements)
  - Existential quantification (some elements)
  - Counting and measuring properties
- **Types**:
  - Universal quantifiers (∀, "for all")
  - Existential quantifiers (∃, "there exists")
  - Restricted quantifiers (limited scope)
- **Role**: Fundamental to mathematical logic, enabling precise expression of general truths and existence claims

## Context
In formal sciences, quantification allows the formulation of universal laws and existential statements. It is essential for mathematical proofs, logical reasoning, and defining properties across entire domains or subsets thereof.
