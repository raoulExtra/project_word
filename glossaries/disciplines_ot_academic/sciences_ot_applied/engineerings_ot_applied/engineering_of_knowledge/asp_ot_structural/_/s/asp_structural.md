# Structural

## Definition
Structural refers to the arrangement, organization, and configuration of components within a system, focusing on how parts are positioned, connected, and structured to form coherent wholes with definable properties and relationships.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Define and analyze the spatial, logical, and organizational arrangements that give systems their form and enable their function
- **Key aspects**:
  - Component relationships (how parts connect and interact)
  - Hierarchical organization (nested levels of structure)
  - Spatial arrangement (physical or conceptual positioning)
  - Logical structure (formal relationships and dependencies)
- **Types**:
  - Physical structure (tangible arrangement of matter)
  - Abstract structure (conceptual or mathematical organization)
  - Dynamic structure (changing configurations over time)
  - Static structure (fixed or stable arrangement)
- **Role**: Provides the framework for understanding how system properties emerge from component arrangements and how structural changes affect system behavior and capabilities

## Context
Structural analysis distinguishes between the form of a system (its structure) and its activity (its function). While functional aspects describe what a system does, structural aspects describe how it is organized to do it. In ontology engineering, structural patterns define how concepts relate and how knowledge is organized for reasoning and inference.
