# Ontology

## Definition
An ontology is a formal, explicit specification of a shared conceptualization that describes the types of entities, their properties, and the relationships among them within a domain of interest.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Provide a structured, machine-readable representation of knowledge
- **Key aspects**:
  - **Concepts**: Classes, entities, or object types
  - **Properties**: Attributes and characteristics of entities
  - **Relationships**: Connections between concepts
  - **Axioms**: Constraints and rules governing the domain
- **Types**:
  - Domain ontologies (specific to a domain)
  - Application ontologies (tailored for specific applications)
  - Upper ontologies (general, cross-domain)
  - Hybrid ontologies (combination of above)
- **Role**: Foundation for knowledge representation, semantic web, AI systems, and information integration

## Context
In ontology engineering, ontologies enable automated reasoning, knowledge sharing, and interoperability across systems. They build upon core concepts like entity, property, and relation to create structured knowledge bases.

**Related terms**: entity, property, relation, domain, context, pattern, component
