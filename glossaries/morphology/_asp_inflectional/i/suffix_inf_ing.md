# -ing (Present Participle/Gerund)

## Definition
The suffix **-ing** is an inflectional/derivational suffix that forms present participles and gerunds.

## Details
- **Function**: Present participle (ongoing action), gerund (noun form of verb)
- **Examples**:
  - **run** → **running** (present participle)
  - **swimming** (gerund: I enjoy swimming)

## Pattern
- **Present participle**: Add -ing to base verb
- **Gerund**: Same form, functions as noun
- **Vowel doubling**: Single vowel before double consonant (run → running)
- **Consonant changes**: -e dropped (make → making)
