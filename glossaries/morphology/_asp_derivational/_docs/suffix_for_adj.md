# Adjective Suffixes in English

## Overview
English adjective suffixes are derivational morphemes that transform base words into adjective forms, expressing various semantic relationships like quality, state, manner, or possession.

## Classification

### Quality/State Suffixes
- **-ful** - full of, characterized by
- **-less** - without, lacking
- **-ous** - full of, characterized by (often emotional/qualitative)

### Manner Suffixes
- **-ly** - in a manner of, having the quality of

### Approximation/Similarity Suffixes
- **-ish** - like, somewhat, approximately

### Possession/Agency Suffixes
- **-or** - one who performs (agent nouns)
- **-en** - conversion suffix
- **-er** - one who performs (agent nouns, verb-to-noun)

### Verb-forming Suffixes
- **-al** - relates to
- **-ify** - make or become
- **-ize/-ise** - make or cause to be
- **-izer** - one who makes
