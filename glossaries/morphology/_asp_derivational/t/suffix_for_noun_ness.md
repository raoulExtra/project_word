# -ness

## Definition
The suffix **-ness** is a derivational suffix that transforms adjectives into nouns, meaning "state or quality of," "condition of," or "characteristic of."

## Details
- **Part of speech**: Suffix (derivational morpheme)
- **Function**: State noun creator - creates nouns expressing states or qualities
- **Semantic effect**: Indicates state, condition, or quality
- **Examples**:
  - **happy** → **happiness** (state of being happy)
  - **dark** → **darkness** (state of being dark)
  - **care** → **careness** (quality of caring)

## Pattern
- **Input**: Adjective root
- **Output**: Noun expressing state or quality

## Context
In morphology and word formation studies, -ness represents a productive suffix that creates abstract nouns expressing emotional or qualitative states.
