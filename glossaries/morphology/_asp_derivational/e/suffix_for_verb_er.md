# -er

## Definition
The suffix **-er** is a derivational suffix in English that transforms verbs into nouns, meaning "one who performs" or "that which performs" the action specified by the root.

## Details
- **Part of speech**: Suffix (derivational morpheme)
- **Function**: Agent noun creator - identifies performer or doer
- **Semantic effect**: Indicates the actor performing an action
- **Examples**:
  - **teach** → **teacher** (one who teaches)
  - **write** → **writer** (one who writes)
  - **build** → **builder** (one who builds)
  - **compute** → **computer** (one who computes, originally)

## Pattern
- **Input**: Verb root
- **Output**: Agent noun expressing the performer
- **Note**: Often creates professional or role identifiers

## Context
In morphology and word formation studies, -er represents a productive pattern that creates agent nouns identifying people or things that perform actions.
