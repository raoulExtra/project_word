# Transcendental

## Definition
Transcendental refers to that which transcends or goes beyond the physical world, ordinary experience, or empirical investigation, addressing fundamental conditions of possibility for any knowledge or experience.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Investigate the preconditions for knowledge and experience
- **Key aspects**:
  - Conditions of possibility (what must be true for knowledge to be possible)
  - A priori knowledge (independent of experience)
  - Synthetic a priori (necessarily true yet informative)
- **Types**:
  - Transcendental unity (of apperception)
  - Transcendental deduction (argument for the self)
  - Transcendental idealism (phenomena vs noumena)
- **Role**: Bridges metaphysics and epistemology, establishing the framework for all possible knowledge

## Context
In metaphysics, transcendental inquiry (especially in Kantian philosophy) examines what must be the case for experience and knowledge to be possible. It transcends the limits of possible experience to investigate the conditions that make objective knowledge possible.
