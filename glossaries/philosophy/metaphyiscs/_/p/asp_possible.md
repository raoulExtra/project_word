# Possible

## Definition
Possible refers to that which is contingently realizable, existing in some complete way of things (world) even if not actual in the present or any actual world. It denotes the realm of what could be the case.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Characterize the full range of alternatives to actuality
- **Key aspects**:
  - Modal possibility (could be the case)
  - Logical possibility (consistent with laws of logic)
  - Metaphysical possibility (consistent with metaphysical laws)
  - Epistemic possibility (consistent with what we know)
- **Types**:
  - Metaphysical possibility (what follows from metaphysical laws)
  - Physical possibility (what follows from physical laws)
  - Logical possibility (what avoids contradiction)
  - Nominal possibility (what can be named/conceived)
- **Role**: Essential to modal logic, counterfactual reasoning, and the analysis of necessity and contingency

## Context
In metaphysics, the concept of possibility is fundamental to modal logic and the semantics of possibility and necessity. Possible worlds semantics provides the standard framework for analyzing modal claims, while debates continue over the nature and ontological status of possible worlds.
