# Semantic

## Definition
Semantic refers to the study of meaning in language, encompassing how words, phrases, sentences, and texts convey meaning through their relationship to reality, other linguistic expressions, and context of use.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Understand how meaning is constructed, conveyed, and interpreted in human language
- **Key aspects**:
  - Lexical semantics (meaning of individual words)
  - Compositional semantics (how meanings combine in phrases and sentences)
  - Reference (relationship between language and world)
  - Sense and reference (distinction between meaning and referent)
- **Types**:
  - Natural semantics (meaning in everyday language)
  - Formal semantics (mathematical and logical approaches)
  - Computational semantics (machine processing of meaning)
  - Pragmatic semantics (context-dependent meaning)
- **Role**: Bridges linguistic expression with intended meaning, enabling precise analysis of how language represents reality and communicates ideas

## Context
In philosophical logic, semantic analysis is essential for ensuring that logical expressions accurately capture the meanings they intend to represent. Semantic precision prevents equivocation in arguments and ensures that logical relationships mirror genuine conceptual connections rather than mere surface similarities.
