# Domain

## Definition
In ontology engineering, a domain is a distinct area of knowledge, application, or subject matter that defines a bounded scope for concepts, relationships, and axioms within a knowledge representation system.

## Details
- **Ontological role**: A categorization mechanism for organizing knowledge
- **Purpose**: Enables modular design and separation of concerns in ontologies
- **Key aspects**:
  - **Subject domain**: Academic or professional field (e.g., medicine, engineering)
  - **Application domain**: Specific use case or problem space
  - **Technical domain**: System or technology context
- **Boundaries**: Clear demarcation of what concepts and relationships belong in the domain

## Context
In structural ontology engineering, domains provide the foundational organization for knowledge systems, enabling coherent representation of specialized vocabularies and their interrelations.
