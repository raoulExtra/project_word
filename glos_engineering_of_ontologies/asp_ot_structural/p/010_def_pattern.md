# Pattern

## Definition
In ontology engineering, a pattern is a reusable template or structure for modeling specific domain concepts, relationships, or design solutions that can be consistently applied across different knowledge systems.

## Details
- **Ontological role**: A standardized solution to a recurring modeling problem
- **Purpose**: Enables consistent, predictable knowledge representation and accelerates ontology development
- **Key aspects**:
  - **Structure**: Defined elements, relationships, and constraints
  - **Reusability**: Applicable across multiple domains or contexts
  - **Documentation**: Clear specification of intent, scope, and application
- **Types**: Design patterns, architectural patterns, modeling patterns

## Context
In structural ontology engineering, patterns provide a bridge between abstract conceptual frameworks and concrete implementations, ensuring coherence and interoperability across knowledge graphs.
