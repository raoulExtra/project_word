# Reasoning

## Definition
Reasoning is the cognitive process of thought and analysis that enables the generation of conclusions, judgments, or predictions from facts, beliefs, or premises, following logical principles and inferential patterns.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Derive conclusions, solve problems, and make decisions through logical analysis
- **Key aspects**:
  - Deductive reasoning (certain conclusions from premises)
  - Inductive reasoning (probable conclusions from evidence)
  - Abductive reasoning (best explanation from observations)
- **Types**:
  - Formal reasoning (follows strict logical rules)
  - Informal reasoning (based on everyday logic and context)
  - Critical reasoning (evaluates assumptions and validity)
- **Role**: Fundamental cognitive capacity that enables knowledge acquisition, problem-solving, and philosophical inquiry

## Context
In philosophy, reasoning is the primary tool for analyzing concepts, constructing arguments, and exploring logical relationships. It underlies all philosophical methodologies from metaphysics to ethics. In cognitive science, reasoning studies the psychological processes behind human thought and judgment.
