# Philosophy Guide

Fundamental concepts and aspects in philosophical inquiry.

## Overview

Philosophy is the systematic study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values, reason, mind, and language. It addresses problems at the foundations of all disciplines.

## Core Concepts

- **Reasoning**: The cognitive process of generating conclusions from premises
- **Knowledge**: Justified true belief about the world
- **Truth**: The property of being accurate or factually correct
- **Value**: Importance, worth, or significance of things

## Aspects

- **Philosophical**: Pertaining to fundamental questions and methods
- **Critical**: Evaluating assumptions, validity, and soundness
- **Foundational**: Providing basic principles and assumptions
- **Informal**: Everyday reasoning outside formal systems
- **Deductive**: Reasoning from general to specific with certainty
- **Inductive**: Reasoning from specific to general with probability
- **Abductive**: Reasoning to the best explanation

## Key Areas

- **Metaphysics**: Nature of reality and existence
- **Epistemology**: Theory of knowledge
- **Ethics**: Moral values and principles
- **Logic**: Formal and informal reasoning patterns
- **Aesthetics**: Nature of beauty and art
