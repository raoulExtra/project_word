# Fact

## Definition
A fact is a statement or proposition that is true and can be verified or substantiated, representing a state of affairs in the world. Facts are the basic units of knowledge and empirical investigation.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Provide the foundational elements for knowledge claims and scientific inquiry
- **Key aspects**:
  - Truth (correspondence to reality)
  - Verification (evidence supporting the claim)
  - Objectivity (independent of subjective belief)
  - Context-dependence (facts relative to frameworks)
- **Types**:
  - Empirical fact (verifiable through observation or experience)
  - Logical fact (true by logical structure)
  - Historical fact (pertaining to past events)
  - Propositional fact (statements about the world)
- **Role**: Essential to epistemology, logic, and the philosophy of science; forms the basis for knowledge and argumentation

## Context
In philosophy, facts are studied in relation to their epistemic status (how we know them), their ontological status (whether they exist independently), and their relationship to beliefs and propositions. The fact/justification debate concerns whether facts are inscrutable or whether our beliefs about them can be justified.
