# Foundational

## Definition
Foundational refers to the basic principles, assumptions, or elements that serve as the foundation for a system of thought, knowledge, or practice. It denotes what is taken as primary or underlying in philosophical inquiry.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Identify and examine core premises and first principles
- **Key aspects**:
  - Underlying assumptions (axioms, postulates)
  - Basic concepts that must be accepted before further reasoning
  - Ground-level elements supporting higher-order structures
- **Types**:
  - Foundational axioms (self-evident truths)
  - Foundational beliefs (core convictions)
  - Foundational practices (basic methods)
- **Role**: Provides the necessary base for building complex theories and systems

## Context
In philosophy, foundational concepts are essential for establishing coherent frameworks across metaphysics, epistemology, ethics, and other branches. They represent what must be accepted or understood before more advanced philosophical inquiry can proceed.
