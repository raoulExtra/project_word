# Concrete

## Definition
Concrete refers to that which has actual existence or spatial-temporal reality, as opposed to abstract or hypothetical entities. It denotes things that are physically real and located in space and time.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Distinguish real entities from conceptual or hypothetical ones
- **Key aspects**:
  - Physical existence (material, spatiotemporal)
  - Temporal location (occurring at specific times)
  - Causal efficacy (able to affect other things)
  - Immediate experience (directly perceivable)
- **Types**:
  - Physical objects (bodies, materials)
  - Events (occurrences in time)
  - States of affairs (actual configurations)
  - Concrete particulars (individual instances)
- **Role**: Fundamental to ontology and the philosophy of mind, distinguishing real entities from universals, sets, or propositions

## Context
In metaphysics and epistemology, the concrete-abstract distinction is crucial for questions of ontological commitment. Physicalists hold that only concrete entities exist, while other positions allow for abstract entities like numbers, properties, or propositions.
