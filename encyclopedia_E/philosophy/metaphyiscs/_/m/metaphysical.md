# Metaphysical

## Definition
Metaphysical refers to that which belongs to or lies at the basis of reality, addressing fundamental questions about the nature of being, existence, time, space, causation, and the relationship between mind and matter.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Investigate the fundamental structure of reality beyond empirical description
- **Key aspects**:
  - Ontology (what exists)
  - Cosmology (structure of the universe)
  - Cosmopsychism (reality as a whole)
  - Transcendental arguments (necessary truths)
- **Types**:
  - Metaphysical necessity (true in all possible worlds)
  - Metaphysical possibility (true in some possible worlds)
  - Metaphysical laws (fundamental principles of being)
  - Metaphysical grounding (deep explanatory structure)
- **Role**: Provides the conceptual framework for understanding reality's most fundamental features

## Context
In metaphysics, the metaphysical domain encompasses all questions about the ultimate structure of reality, including the nature of time, causation, personal identity, modality, and the relationship between abstract and concrete entities. It distinguishes metaphysical facts from empirical facts discoverable through scientific investigation.
