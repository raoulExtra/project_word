# Metaphysics

## Definition
Metaphysics is the branch of philosophy that investigates the fundamental nature of reality, including the relationship between mind and matter, substance and attribute, potentiality and actuality. It addresses questions that lie at the foundation of all scientific and ordinary knowledge.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Understand the ultimate structure and principles underlying all that exists
- **Key aspects**:
  - Ontology (the nature of being and existence)
  - Cosmology (the structure of the universe)
  - Modality (possibility, necessity, and contingency)
  - Identity and change (persistence through time)
- **Types**:
  - General metaphysics (core questions about reality)
  - Special metaphysics (focused investigations, e.g., philosophy of mind)
  - Formal metaphysics (logical and conceptual analysis)
  - Applied metaphysics (metaphysical implications of scientific theories)
- **Role**: Provides the conceptual framework for all empirical investigation and the foundation for understanding the deepest meanings of existence

## Context
Metaphysics stands at the intersection of philosophy and science, informing and being informed by developments in physics, cognitive science, and mathematics. It addresses perennial questions about time, space, causation, and the nature of objects while engaging with contemporary debates about simulation, virtuality, and the fabric of spacetime.
