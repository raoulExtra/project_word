# Identity

## Definition
Identity is the relation that a thing bears to itself, making it the same thing over time and across contexts. It concerns what makes an entity numerically identical with itself rather than merely similar to itself.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Determine what makes an object the same over time and change
- **Key aspects**:
  - Numerical identity (one and only one entity)
  - Qualitative identity (sharing all properties)
  - Transitivity (if A = B and B = C, then A = C)
  - Reflexivity (everything equals itself)
- **Types**:
  - Personal identity (what makes a person the same person)
  - Material identity (physical continuity)
  - Psychological identity (mental/behavioral continuity)
  - Essential identity (core nature or essence)
- **Role**: Fundamental to ontology, logic, and philosophical analysis of persistence and change

## Context
In metaphysics, identity is central to questions of personal survival, material constitution, and the nature of change. The identity of indiscernibles is a key principle, while puzzles like the Ship of Theseus challenge our understanding of persistence through change.
