# Existential

## Definition
Existential refers to the quality or state of being an existing entity, particularly concerning the fundamental question of what it means for something to exist, the nature of existence itself, and the relationship between existence and meaning.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Examine the nature, structure, and implications of existence
- **Key aspects**:
  - Ontological commitment (what we must say exists)
  - Modes of existence (actual vs possible vs abstract)
  - Existential quantification (logical "there exists")
- **Types**:
  - Simple existence (being present in reality)
  - Derived existence (existing as a property of substances)
  - Negative existence (the non-existent, fictional entities)
- **Role**: Bridges ontological analysis with logical and linguistic expression of existence claims

## Context
In metaphysics, existential aspects are central to questions about what exists, how we can know what exists, and the relationship between existence and essence. Existential themes are fundamental to both analytical metaphysics and continental philosophy traditions.
