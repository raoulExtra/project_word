# Existence

## Definition
Existence is the state or condition of being real, actual, or present in the world, distinct from mere possibility, potentiality, or conceptual construction.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Investigate the fundamental nature of what it means for something to exist
- **Key aspects**:
  - Ontological status (what kinds of things exist)
  - Modes of existence (physical, abstract, possible, actual)
  - Problems of reference (how we talk about non-existent things)
- **Types**:
  - Physical existence (material, spatiotemporal beings)
  - Abstract existence (numbers, sets, properties)
  - Possible existence (things that could exist but don't)
  - Negative existence (the non-existent, fictional entities)
- **Role**: Central to metaphysical inquiry, addressing the most basic question of what there is

## Context
In metaphysics, existence is the primary subject of ontological investigation. Philosophers debate whether to accept existence as a predicate, how to account for negative existentials, and the relationship between existence and essence. Existence questions underlie all metaphysical analysis.
