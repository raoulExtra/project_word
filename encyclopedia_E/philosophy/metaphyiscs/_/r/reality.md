# Reality

## Definition
Reality is the state of things as they actually exist, as opposed to ideas, concepts, or appearances. It encompasses the totality of existence, including both the physical world and the structures that govern it.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Establish the fundamental nature of existence as opposed to mere appearance or thought
- **Key aspects**:
  - Ontological status (what exists vs what merely appears)
  - Physical vs mental (material existence vs conceptual constructs)
  - Objective vs subjective (independent vs perspective-dependent)
- **Types**:
  - Physical reality (material, spatiotemporal existence)
  - Social reality (constructed through social conventions)
  - Mental reality (psychological, experiential existence)
- **Role**: The foundational domain of metaphysical inquiry, addressing what there is independent of human thought

## Context
In metaphysics, reality is the primary subject of ontological investigation. Questions about the nature of reality underlie all philosophical inquiry, from the classical problems of mind-body dualism to contemporary debates about simulation, virtuality, and the fabric of spacetime.
