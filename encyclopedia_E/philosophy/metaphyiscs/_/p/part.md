# Part

## Definition
Part refers to a portion or component of a whole, understood in terms of mereological relationships. It denotes the relationship between entities and their constituent elements, whether physical, abstract, or conceptual.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Analyze the nature of composition and the relationships between wholes and their constituents
- **Key aspects**:
  - Mereological sum (the sum of parts)
  - Fusion (combining parts into a whole)
  - Parthood (the relation holding between a part and its whole)
  - Overlap (sharing some parts)
- **Types**:
  - Proper part (a part that is not identical to the whole)
  - Improper part (a part that is identical to the whole)
  - Maximal part (a part with no proper parts of its own)
  - Minimal part (an indivisible part)
- **Role**: Essential to mereology, metaphysical composition problems, and the analysis of identity and change

## Context
In metaphysics, mereological principles inform debates about material constitution, temporal parts, and the nature of objects. The part-whole relationship is fundamental to understanding how complexes are built from simples and how identity persists through change.
