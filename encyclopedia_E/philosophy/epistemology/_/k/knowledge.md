# Knowledge

## Definition
Knowledge is the state or quality of knowing facts, information, or skills acquired through experience, learning, or reasoning. It is traditionally defined as justified true belief, though this definition faces significant philosophical challenges.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Distinguish genuine knowledge from mere opinion, belief, or information
- **Key aspects**:
  - Truth (correspondence to reality)
  - Justification (warranted belief)
  - Reliability (consistent acquisition)
  - Safety (protection from error)
- **Types**:
  - Empirical knowledge (from sensory experience)
  - A Priori knowledge (independent of experience)
  - Propositional knowledge (knowledge that)
  - Tacit knowledge (implicit, hard to verbalize)
  - Extended knowledge (distributed across tools and environment)
- **Role**: Central to epistemology, foundational to all inquiry, and essential to rational discourse

## Context
In philosophy, knowledge is the primary object of epistemology, which investigates its nature, possibility, sources, and limits. The traditional definition as "justified true belief" has been challenged by Gettier problems, leading to sophisticated alternative theories including reliabilist, causal, and virtue-theoretic accounts. In cognitive science, knowledge is studied as a psychological capacity for recall, recognition, and application.
