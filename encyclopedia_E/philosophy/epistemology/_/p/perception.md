# Perception

## Definition
Perception is the process by which organisms interpret and organize sensory information to understand and navigate the world. It is the primary gateway to empirical knowledge.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Transform sensory input into meaningful information about the environment
- **Key aspects**:
  - Sensory modalities (sight, hearing, touch, etc.)
  - Direct realism (perception as direct access to reality)
  - Representationalism (perception as mental representation)
  - Constructive aspects (active interpretation)
- **Types**:
  - Empirical perception (from sensory experience)
  - Introspective perception (of one's own mental states)
  - Social perception (interpreting others' actions and intentions)
- **Role**: Primary source of empirical knowledge and the foundation of sensory-based belief formation

## Context
In epistemology, perception is studied as the main route to empirical knowledge. Philosophical questions focus on the reliability of perceptual beliefs, the relationship between perception and reality, and the nature of perceptual experience.
