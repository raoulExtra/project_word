# Evidence

## Definition
Evidence is the available body of facts, information, or signs that support or constitute the grounds for belief, knowledge, or justification. It serves as the material basis for epistemic evaluation and knowledge claims.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Provide the foundation for knowledge acquisition and belief formation
- **Key aspects**:
  - Empirical evidence (from observation and experience)
  - Testimonial evidence (from other sources)
  - Logical evidence (deductive or inductive support)
  - Circumstantial evidence (contextual support)
- **Types**:
  - Direct evidence (immediate perception)
  - Indirect evidence (inferential support)
  - Conclusive evidence (fully supporting a claim)
  - Prima facie evidence (initially supporting, subject to revision)
- **Role**: Essential to epistemology, scientific methodology, and the justification of beliefs

## Context
In epistemology, evidence is studied in relation to its epistemic value, sources, and role in justification. The evidentialist thesis holds that justification depends entirely on evidence. In science, evidence must be empirical and publicly accessible, while in law, evidence standards vary by context.
