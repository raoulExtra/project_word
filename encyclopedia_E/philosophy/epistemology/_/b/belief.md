# Belief

## Definition
Belief is a mental state in which an individual accepts a proposition as true, whether or not it actually is true. It represents acceptance of a claim as significant or meaningful.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Enable cognitive processing and decision-making about the world
- **Key aspects**:
  - Propositional (about a specific state of affairs)
  - Persuasion (degree of confidence)
  - Acceptance (mental assent)
  - Disposition (tendency to act on the belief)
- **Types**:
  - Sensory belief (based on perception)
  - Doctrinal belief (based on teaching or authority)
  - Hypothetical belief (counterfactual or speculative)
  - Implicit belief (unstated but acted upon)
- **Role**: Fundamental to the analysis of knowledge (as "justified true belief") and central to philosophy of mind

## Context
In epistemology, beliefs are the raw material for knowledge claims. The relationship between belief and knowledge is central to questions of epistemic luck, the psychology of belief formation, and the norms governing rational belief.
