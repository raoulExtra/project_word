# Truth

## Definition
Truth is the property of propositions that correspond to reality, accurately representing how things actually are. It is the fundamental goal and norm of epistemic inquiry.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Establish the standard for accurate belief and knowledge
- **Key aspects**:
  - Correspondence (matching reality)
  - Coherence (consistency within belief systems)
  - Pragmatic (useful consequences)
  - Deflationary (minimal ontological commitment)
- **Types**:
  - Factual truth (about the world)
  - Logical truth (true by form)
  - Epistemic truth (what is known to be true)
  - Aesthetic truth (artistic correctness)
- **Role**: Central to epistemology, providing the normative standard for knowledge

## Context
In epistemology, truth is studied as the ultimate epistemic goal. The correspondence theory dominates analytic philosophy, while coherence theories are prominent in holistic traditions. Deflationary theories argue that truth has no substantive nature beyond "being true."
