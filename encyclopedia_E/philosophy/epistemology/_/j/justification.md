# Justification

## Definition
Justification is the reason or evidence supporting a belief, providing the epistemic ground for accepting a proposition as knowledge rather than mere opinion. It answers the question: "Why believe this?"

## Details
- **Purpose**: Establish the rational grounds for accepting beliefs as knowledge
- **Key aspects**:
  - Foundationalism (basic beliefs supporting derived beliefs)
  - Coherentism (mutual support among beliefs)
  - Infinitism (infinite regress of reasons)
  - Reliabilism (reliable belief-forming processes)
- **Types**:
  - Epistemic justification (rational support)
  - Practical justification (prudential reasons)
  - Moral justification (ethical grounds)
- **Role**: Essential to the analysis of knowledge as "justified true belief"

## Context
In epistemology, justification is central to the traditional analysis of knowledge. The Gettier problem showed that justification alone is insufficient for knowledge, leading to sophisticated theories like reliabilism and virtue epistemology that focus on the reliability or skill underlying beliefs.
