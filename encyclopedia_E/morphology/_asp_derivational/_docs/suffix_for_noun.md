# Noun Suffixes in English

## Overview
English noun suffixes are derivational morphemes that transform base words (primarily roots, adjectives, or verbs) into noun forms, expressing various semantic relationships like person, place, thing, or concept.

## Classification

### Person/Role Suffixes
- **-er** - one who performs (teacher, writer, builder)
- **-or** - one who performs (actor, navigator, inventor)
- **-ee** - one who is acted upon (attendee, refugee)
- **-ist** - one who practices or specializes (artist, biologist, musician)
- **-ian** - native of, relating to (American, Canadian, Parisian)

### Place/Thing Suffixes
- **-er** - agent noun (tool, device)
- **-or** - instrument (instrument, vehicle)
- **-ive** - tendon, retina (body parts)
- **-ine** - medicine (morphine, cocaine)
- **-ic** - state, condition (panic, music)

### Concept/Object Suffixes
- **-tion/-sion** - act or state (action, decision, confusion)
- **-ness** - state or quality (happiness, darkness)
- **-ity/-ness** - state (quality, hardness)
- **-ship** - state or condition (leadership, citizenship)
- **-hood** - state or membership (childhood, manhood)
- **-dom** - state, rank, or domain (freedom, domain, earldom)

### Diminutive/Affectionate Suffixes
- **-let** - small (booklet, leaflet)
- **-kin** - kind (collie, shawl)
- **-ette** - small (kitchenette, statuette)
- **-ling** - small, young (duckling, fledgling)
