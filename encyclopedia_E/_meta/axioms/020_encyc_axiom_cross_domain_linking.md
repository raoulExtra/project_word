## Axiom

Concepts that exist across multiple domains under focus must be explicitly linked through cross-domain references in their canonical articles.

## Rationale

While each concept has one canonical article (see `010_encyc_axiom_concept_identity.md`), concepts often span multiple domains. Without explicit linking, domain-specific knowledge silos emerge.

This axiom ensures:
- Cross-domain relationships are made explicit
- Users can discover domain-specific nuances of a concept
- AI systems can traverse concepts across domains without ambiguity
- Domain-specific contexts are preserved without fragmentation

## Implementation

- Canonical articles for cross-domain concepts must include a "Cross-Domain References" section
- Each reference must specify the domain and the nature of the relationship
- Links must be bidirectional where appropriate
