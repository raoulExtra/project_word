# Degree

## Definition
A degree is a unit of measurement that expresses the intensity, extent, or magnitude of a quantitative attribute, providing a standardized way to compare and evaluate characteristics.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Quantify the intensity or extent of attributes
- **Key aspects**:
  - **Measurement scale**: Defines the range and increments
  - **Standardization**: Consistent units for comparison
  - **Precision**: Level of detail in measurement
- **Types**:
  - **Ordinal degrees**: Ranked categories (low, medium, high)
  - **Interval degrees**: Equal intervals between values
  - **Ratio degrees**: Absolute zero point with proportional relationships
- **Role**: Enables fine-grained quantification and meaningful comparison of attribute intensities

## Context
In engineering of knowledge, degrees provide the granularity needed for precise attribute specification. They transform qualitative assessments into quantifiable metrics, supporting automated reasoning and decision-making.

**Related terms**: quantitative attribute, measurement, scale, magnitude
