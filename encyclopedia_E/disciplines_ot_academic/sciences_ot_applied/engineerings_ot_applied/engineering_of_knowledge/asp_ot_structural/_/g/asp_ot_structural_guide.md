# Structural Ontology Engineering Guide

This guide covers the structural aspects of ontology engineering, including organization, patterns, and context management.

## Overview

Structural ontology engineering focuses on the arrangement, organization, and configuration of ontological components to create coherent, maintainable knowledge systems.

## Key Concepts

### Domain
A distinct area of knowledge with bounded scope for concepts and relationships.

### Context
Environmental factors that determine the applicability and interpretation of knowledge representations.

### Pattern
Reusable templates for modeling specific domain concepts and relationships.

### Structural Organization
The arrangement of components including:
- Hierarchical classification
- Modular decomposition
- Interface boundaries
- Dependency relationships

## Design Principles

1. **Modularity**: Separate concerns into independent modules
2. **Coherence**: Maintain internal consistency within modules
3. **Reusability**: Design patterns for cross-domain application
4. **Scalability**: Support growth and evolution

## Applications

- Knowledge graph construction
- Semantic web technologies
- Database schema design
- System architecture modeling
