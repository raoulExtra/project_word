# Encyclopedia

## Definition
An encyclopedia is a comprehensive, systematic compilation of knowledge that organizes information about diverse subjects into a structured, accessible form. It serves as a repository of human knowledge, presenting established facts, concepts, and understanding in a way that facilitates learning, reference, and discovery.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Preserve, organize, and disseminate accumulated human knowledge and understanding
- **Key aspects**:
  - **Comprehensive**: Attempts to cover a domain or all of human knowledge
  - **Authoritative**: Presents verified, established information
  - **Structured**: Organizes content through classification, indexing, and cross-referencing
  - **Accessible**: Designed for ease of consultation and learning
  - **Systematic**: Follows consistent principles for organization and presentation
- **Types**:
  - **General encyclopedias**: Cover broad domains of knowledge
  - **Specialized encyclopedias**: Focus on specific fields or disciplines
  - **Digital encyclopedias**: Electronic format with search and linking capabilities
  - **Collaborative encyclopedias**: Built through community contribution
- **Role**: Foundation for knowledge transfer, education, and reference across all domains

## Context
In the engineering of knowledge, an encyclopedia represents a deliberate knowledge architecture. It embodies decisions about scope, organization, authority, and accessibility. Modern encyclopedias increasingly integrate computational methods for knowledge representation, enabling automated reasoning and discovery.
