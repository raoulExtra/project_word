# Reusable Terms for Ontology Engineering

This document identifies terms that can be reused from higher-level glossaries for engineering_of_ontologies.

## Core Concepts

### structural
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/_/s/asp_structural.md`

> Structural refers to the arrangement, organization, and configuration of elements within any domain of knowledge or system, focusing on how components are positioned, connected, and structured to form coherent wholes.

**Key aspects**: Component relationships, Hierarchical organization, Spatial arrangement, Logical structure
**Types**: Physical, Abstract, Dynamic, Static structures

### concept
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/concepts_ot_core/_/c/def_concept.md`

> A mental construct, idea, or understanding that represents a general category, property, relation, or entity, serving as a building block for knowledge, reasoning, and communication.

**Key aspects**: Abstraction, Categorization, Reference
**Types**: Universal, Particular, Relational, Abstract concepts

### entity
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/concepts_ot_core/_/e/def_entity.md`

> An entity is a distinct, identifiable object, being, or concept that exists as a unified whole and can be subject to analysis, reference, and manipulation within ontological, logical, or computational frameworks.

**Key aspects**: Distinctness, Persistence, Independence, Types (Physical, Abstract, Social, Digital)

### property
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/_/p/def_property.md`

> A property is an attribute, characteristic, or quality of an object, system, or phenomenon that can be measured, observed, or described.

**Types**: Physical, Chemical, Mathematical properties

### relation / relational
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/concepts_ot_core/_/r/asp_relational.md`

> Relational refers to the quality or state of being a relation, involving connections, dependencies, or interactions between entities, concepts, or systems.

**Types**: Spatial, Temporal, Conceptual, Database relations

## Modal/Metaphysical

### existence
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/philosophy/metaphyiscs/_/e/def_existence.md`

> Existence is the state or condition of being real, actual, or present in the world, distinct from mere possibility, potentiality, or conceptual construction.

**Modes**: Physical, Abstract, Possible, Negative existence

### identity
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/philosophy/metaphyiscs/_/i/def_identity.md`

> Identity is the relation that a thing bears to itself, making it the same thing over time and across contexts.

**Types**: Personal, Material, Psychological, Essential identity

### possible / actual
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/philosophy/metaphyiscs/_/a/asp_actual.md` and `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/philosophy/metaphyiscs/_/p/asp_possible.md`

> Actual refers to that which exists or occurs in fact... Possible refers to that which is contingently realizable...

**Key distinction**: Actual vs Possible, Actualism vs Possibilism

## Formal Logic

### quantification
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_formal/_/q/def_quantification.md`

> Quantification is the process of assigning a quantity or number to elements within a domain, using logical operators to express universal and existential claims.

**Types**: Universal (∀), Existential (∃), Restricted quantification

### valid
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_formal/mathematics/logic/_/v/asp_valid.md`

> Valid refers to the quality or state of arguments, inferences, or reasoning that conform to logical standards ensuring truth preservation.

**Types**: Deductive, Inductive, Abductive validity

### statement / proposition
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/_/s/def_statement.md` and `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_formal/_/p/def_proposition.md`

> A statement is a declarative sentence that is either true or false... A proposition is a declarative statement that expresses a mathematical or logical assertion.

## Structural/Dimensional

### dimension
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/_/d/def_dimension.md`

> A dimension is a fundamental parameter or measurable extent that defines the nature and structure of an object, space, or system.

**Types**: Spatial, Temporal, Abstract dimensions

### space
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/_/s/def_space.md`

> Space is the three-dimensional extent in which all objects and events occur and move, providing the framework for physical location, distance, and geometric relationships.

**Types**: Physical, Spacetime, Abstract, Social space

### stratifikation
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_formal/mathematics/logic/_/s/def_stratifikation.md`

> Stratifikation is the process of arranging or classifying elements into hierarchical or layered structures based on shared characteristics or levels of complexity.

**Applications**: Database normalization, Type systems, Social stratification, Geological layers

## Additional Terms from Ontology Engineering Context

### domain
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_applied/engineerings_ot_applied/engineering_of_knowledge/asp_ot_structural/_/d/010_def_domain.md`

> In ontology engineering, a domain is a distinct area of knowledge, application, or subject matter that defines a bounded scope for concepts, relationships, and axioms within a knowledge representation system.

### context
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_applied/engineerings_ot_applied/engineering_of_knowledge/asp_ot_structural/_/c/010_def_context.md`

> In ontology engineering, context is the set of environmental factors, background conditions, or situational parameters that determine the applicability, interpretation, or relevance of an assertion or knowledge representation.

### pattern
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_applied/engineerings_ot_applied/engineering_of_knowledge/asp_ot_structural/_/p/010_def_pattern.md`

> In ontology engineering, a pattern is a reusable template or structure for modeling specific domain concepts, relationships, or design solutions that can be consistently applied across different knowledge systems.

### structural
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_applied/engineerings_ot_applied/engineering_of_knowledge/asp_ot_structural/_/s/asp_structural.md`

> Structural refers to the arrangement, organization, and configuration of components within a system, focusing on how parts are positioned, connected, and structured to form coherent wholes.

**Key aspects**: Component relationships, Hierarchical organization, Spatial arrangement, Logical structure

### aspect
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_ot_applied/engineerings_ot_applied/engineering_of_knowledge/asp_ot_structural/_/a/010_def_aspect.md`

> In linguistics and ontology engineering, aspect is a grammatical category that expresses the temporal structure of a situation or event, distinguishing between ongoing, completed, habitual, or repetitive actions.

**Ontological role**: Structural dimension for modeling temporal and event-based relationships

## Systems/Components

### component
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_for_systems/_/c/def_component.md`

> A component is a distinct, identifiable part of a system that contributes to the system's overall function, structure, or behavior. Components are interconnected to form composite systems.

**Key aspects**: Modularity, Interoperability, Interface, Behavior
**Types**: Hardware, Software, Functional, Organizational components

### system
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_for_systems/_/s/def_system.md`

> A set of interrelated components working together to achieve a common goal.

**Key aspects**: Components, Interactions, Boundaries, Inputs/Outputs
**Types**: Natural, Artificial, Social, Technical systems

### functional
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_for_systems/_/f/asp_functional.md`

> Functional refers to the purpose, role, or operation of a system component within the broader system context.

**Key aspects**: System functions, Functional architecture, Performance requirements, Functional dependencies
**Types**: Declared, Actual, Emergent functions

### organizational
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/sciences_for_systems/_/o/asp_organizational.md`

> The structure and procedures of an organization, including the arrangement of components and their relationships.

**Key aspects**: Hierarchy, Roles, Responsibilities, Communication channels
**Types**: Formal, Informal, Matrix organizations

## Complexity

### complexity
From: `glossaries/disciplines_ot_academic/concepts_ot_core/_/c/complexity/asp_complexity.md`

> Complexity refers to the degree to which a system, concept, or structure exhibits intricate relationships, emergent properties, and non-linear behaviors that arise from the interactions of its components.

**Key aspects**: Emergence, Non-linearity, Self-organization, Adaptive behavior
**Types**: Simple, Complex, Dynamic, Static complexity
