# Logical

## Definition
Logical refers to the quality or state of being governed by principles of reasoning and inference. It denotes characteristics related to logic as a formal science dealing with valid argumentation, truth preservation, and structural relationships between propositions.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Identify properties and applications within logical frameworks
- **Key aspects**:
  - Valid inference (preservation of truth)
  - Propositional structure (statements and connectives)
  - Deductive reasoning (certainty from premises)
- **Types**:
  - Logical operators (and, or, not, if-then)
  - Logical forms (valid argument patterns)
  - Logical systems (formal frameworks)
- **Role**: Provides the rules and structures for correct reasoning, enabling valid deduction, proof construction, and systematic analysis of relationships between concepts

## Context
In formal sciences, logical aspects distinguish problems requiring rigorous argumentation and truth preservation. They encompass all branches of logic from propositional to predicate calculus, providing universal frameworks for mathematical proofs, computer science verification, philosophy, and scientific methodology. Logical reasoning relies on axioms, definitions, inference rules, and theorems to establish validity within defined systems.
