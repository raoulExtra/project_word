# Primary

## Definition
Primary refers to the fundamental, essential, or basic elements that constitute the core foundation of a system, theory, or discipline. It denotes what is taken as principal or most important in its context.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Identify and prioritize core elements over secondary or derivative ones
- **Key aspects**:
  - Fundamental building blocks (as opposed to composite)
  - Essential characteristics defining the nature of a system
  - Priority ordering within theoretical frameworks
- **Types**:
  - Primary causes (root origins)
  - Primary qualities (intrinsic properties)
  - Primary substances (fundamental entities)
- **Role**: Establishes the foundational layer upon which secondary and derived concepts are built

## Context
In formal sciences, primary aspects distinguish core principles from auxiliary or derivative elements. They represent what must be understood first before more complex or specialized concepts can be properly grasped.
