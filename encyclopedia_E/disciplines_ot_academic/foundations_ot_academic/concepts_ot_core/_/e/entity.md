# Entity

## Definition
An entity is a distinct, identifiable object, being, or concept that exists as a unified whole and can be subject to analysis, reference, and manipulation within ontological, logical, or computational frameworks.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Provide a fundamental unit of analysis and modeling
- **Key aspects**:
  - Distinctness (clearly bounded and identifiable)
  - Persistence (maintains identity over time)
  - Independence (can exist separately from others)
- **Types**:
  - Physical entities (objects, organisms, substances)
  - Abstract entities (numbers, sets, propositions)
  - Social entities (organizations, institutions, roles)
  - Digital entities (data objects, virtual constructs)
- **Role**: Serves as the basic building block for ontologies, databases, and conceptual models

## Context
In core concepts, entities are fundamental to ontology, philosophy, computer science, and systems theory. They represent the primary subjects of study and the basic elements that can be related, classified, and manipulated. Entity relationships and properties form the foundation for knowledge representation and information modeling.
