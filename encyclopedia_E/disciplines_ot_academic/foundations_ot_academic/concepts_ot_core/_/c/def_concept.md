# Concept

## Definition
A concept is a mental construct, idea, or understanding that represents a general category, property, relation, or entity, serving as a building block for knowledge, reasoning, and communication.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Provide the fundamental units of thought and knowledge representation
- **Key aspects**:
  - Abstraction (generalization from specific instances)
  - Categorization (grouping similar entities)
  - Reference (standing for objects, properties, or relations)
- **Types**:
  - Universal concepts (classes, categories)
  - Particular concepts (specific instances)
  - Relational concepts (connections between entities)
  - Abstract concepts (non-physical entities like numbers, qualities)
- **Role**: Forms the foundation of ontologies, taxonomies, and conceptual models, enabling systematic organization and reasoning about knowledge

## Context
In core concepts, concepts are the basic elements that can be defined, related, and organized into complex knowledge structures. They bridge the gap between reality and mental representation, allowing for precise communication and logical analysis. Concepts are central to philosophy, cognitive science, linguistics, and information science.
