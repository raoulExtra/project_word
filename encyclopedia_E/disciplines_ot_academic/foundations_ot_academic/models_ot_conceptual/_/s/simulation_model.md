# Simulation Model

## Definition
A simulation model is a computational or mathematical representation designed to imitate the behavior or operation of a real or hypothetical system over time, producing simulated data or events.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Replicate system behavior for analysis, prediction, or experimentation
- **Key aspects**:
  - Time progression (discrete or continuous)
  - State changes (system evolution)
  - Output generation (results collection)
- **Types**:
  - Discrete-event simulation (event-driven)
  - Continuous simulation (differential equations)
  - Agent-based simulation (individual behaviors)
  - Monte Carlo simulation (random sampling)
- **Role**: Enable experimentation without real-world risks or costs

## Context
Simulation models are indispensable in engineering, operations research, and complex systems analysis. They allow testing of policies and designs before implementation.

**Related terms**: model, computation, experiment
