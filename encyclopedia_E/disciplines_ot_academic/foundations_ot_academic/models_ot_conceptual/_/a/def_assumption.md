# Assumption

## Definition
An assumption is a premise or proposition accepted as true without proof or verification, serving as a foundation for building a model or conducting reasoning.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Enable progress when complete information is unavailable or complexity must be reduced
- **Key aspects**:
  - Necessity (required for model construction)
  - Testability (can be validated or refuted)
  - Impact (affects model conclusions)
- **Types**:
  - Working assumptions (provisional, for exploration)
  - Foundational assumptions (core model premises)
  - Simplifying assumptions (reduce complexity)
  - Boundary assumptions (define model limits)
- **Role**: Critical for model construction when complete information is impractical

## Context
Assumptions are unavoidable in modeling. Good modeling practice requires explicit identification and documentation of assumptions to ensure transparency and enable appropriate interpretation of results.

**Related terms**: model, simplification, constraint
