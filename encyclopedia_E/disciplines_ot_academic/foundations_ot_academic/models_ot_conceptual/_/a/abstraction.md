# Abstraction

## Definition
Abstraction is the process of simplifying a complex reality by focusing on essential features while ignoring or suppressing irrelevant details, creating a generalized representation suitable for analysis and reasoning.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Reduce complexity to enable manageable understanding and analysis
- **Key aspects**:
  - Generalization (identifying common patterns)
  - Simplification (removing non-essential details)
  - Focus (highlighting relevant characteristics)
- **Types**:
  - Conceptual abstraction (mental constructs)
  - Spatial abstraction (geometric simplification)
  - Temporal abstraction (time-scale reduction)
  - Functional abstraction (behavior-focused)
- **Role**: Essential technique for creating models and managing complexity

## Context
Abstraction is fundamental to all modeling and knowledge representation. It enables humans to reason about complex systems by focusing on relevant aspects while ignoring distracting details.

**Related terms**: model, simplification, generalization
