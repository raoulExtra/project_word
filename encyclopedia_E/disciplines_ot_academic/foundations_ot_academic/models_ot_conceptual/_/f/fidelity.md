# Fidelity

## Definition
Fidelity refers to the degree to which a model accurately represents the essential characteristics of the system or phenomenon it models, indicating the closeness of the match between the model and reality.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Measure how well a model captures the essential aspects of reality
- **Key aspects**:
  - Structural fidelity (correct relationships)
  - Behavioral fidelity (correct dynamics)
  - Functional fidelity (correct purposes)
- **Types**:
  - High fidelity (detailed, accurate representation)
  - Low fidelity (simplified, approximate)
  - Selective fidelity (high in some aspects, low in others)
- **Role**: Guides model development and selection for specific purposes

## Context
Fidelity is a key consideration in model design. Higher fidelity generally means greater accuracy but also greater complexity. The appropriate level depends on the model's intended use.

**Related terms**: model, accuracy, approximation
