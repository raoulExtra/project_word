# Model

## Definition
A model is an abstract representation of reality, a system, or a phenomenon, constructed to understand, analyze, predict, or design based on simplified assumptions and structural relationships.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Provide simplified representations that enable understanding, analysis, and prediction
- **Key aspects**:
  - Abstraction (removing irrelevant details)
  - Representation (capturing essential structure)
  - Purposefulness (serving specific analytical goals)
- **Types**:
  - Conceptual models (ideas and mental constructs)
  - Physical models (material representations)
  - Mathematical models (equations and formalisms)
  - Computational models (executable simulations)
- **Role**: Fundamental tool for reasoning about complex systems and phenomena

## Context
Models are central to science, engineering, and knowledge representation. They bridge the gap between reality and understanding, enabling systematic analysis and decision-making.

**Related terms**: abstraction, representation, assumption
