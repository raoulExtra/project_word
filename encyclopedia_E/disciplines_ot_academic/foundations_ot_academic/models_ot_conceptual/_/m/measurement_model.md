# Measurement Model

## Definition
A measurement model is a formal specification that defines how measurements are obtained, what they represent, and how they relate to the underlying quantities or properties being measured.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Establish the relationship between observed measurements and theoretical quantities
- **Key aspects**:
  - Instrumentation (measuring devices)
  - Calibration (scale relationships)
  - Uncertainty (measurement error)
- **Types**:
  - Direct measurement models (one-to-one relationship)
  - Indirect measurement models (derived quantities)
  - Probabilistic measurement models (uncertain mappings)
- **Role**: Bridge between empirical data and theoretical constructs

## Context
Measurement models are essential in experimental sciences and engineering. They ensure that measurements meaningfully represent the properties they claim to measure.

**Related terms**: model, quantity, uncertainty
