# Constraint

## Definition
A constraint is a limitation, restriction, or boundary that defines the allowable configurations, behaviors, or values within a model, system, or problem space.

## Details
- **Purpose**: Limit solution space to reflect realistic or desired conditions
- **Key aspects**:
  - Necessity (must be satisfied)
  - Boundary (defines limits)
  - Impact (shapes possible outcomes)
- **Types**:
  - Hard constraints (must always be satisfied)
  - Soft constraints (preferred but allowible to violate)
  - Boundary constraints (define limits)
  - Logical constraints (internal consistency requirements)
- **Role**: Essential for ensuring model validity and practical applicability

## Context
Constraints are fundamental to optimization, design, and system modeling. They encode real-world limitations and requirements, ensuring that models produce feasible and meaningful results.

**Related terms**: model, boundary, assumption
