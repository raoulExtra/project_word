# -ize / -ise

## Definition
The suffixes **-ize** and **-ise** are derivational suffixes in English that transform adjectives, nouns, or other bases into verbs, typically meaning "to make," "to cause to be," or "to turn into."

## Details
- **Part of speech**: Suffix (derivational morpheme)
- **Function**: Verbalizer - creates action words
- **Semantic effect**: Indicates causation, transformation, or state change
- **Examples**:
  - **simple** → **simplify** (make simple)
  - **civil** → **civilize** (make civil)
  - **modern** → **modernize** (make modern)
  - **real** → **realize** (make real)

## Pattern
- **Input**: Adjective, noun, or base
- **Output**: Verb expressing action or state change
- **Variation**: -ize (American) vs -ise (British)

## Context
In morphology and word formation studies, -ize/-ise represents a highly productive verb-forming pattern, especially common in academic, technical, and international vocabulary.
