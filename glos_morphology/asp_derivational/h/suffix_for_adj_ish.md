# -ish

## Definition
The suffix **-ish** is a derivational suffix in English that transforms adjectives into adjectives, meaning "of, belonging to," "having the nature of," or "approximately."

## Details
- **Part of speech**: Suffix (derivational morpheme)
- **Function**: Approximator - creates descriptive or approximate forms
- **Semantic effect**: Indicates similarity, approximation, or mild intensity
- **Examples**:
  - **child** → **childish** (like a child)
  - **boy** → **boyish** (like a boy)
  - **red** → **reddish** (somewhat red)
  - **tall** → **tallish** (somewhat tall)

## Pattern
- **Input**: Noun or adjective root
- **Output**: Adjective meaning "like" or "somewhat"
- **Note**: Can indicate approximation or childish qualities

## Context
In morphology and word formation studies, -ish represents a productive suffix that creates adjectives expressing similarity or approximation.
