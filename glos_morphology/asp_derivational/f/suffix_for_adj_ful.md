# -ful

## Definition
The suffix **-ful** is a derivational suffix in English that transforms adjectives into adjectives, meaning "full of" or "characterized by" the quality specified by the root.

## Details
- **Part of speech**: Suffix (derivational morpheme)
- **Function**: Possessor - creates quality-describing adjectives
- **Semantic effect**: Indicates fullness or presence of a quality
- **Examples**:
  - **joy** → **joyful** (full of joy)
  - **fear** → **fearful** (full of fear)
  - **hope** → **hopeful** (full of hope)
  - **care** → **careful** (full of care)

## Pattern
- **Input**: Noun or adjective root
- **Output**: Adjective meaning "full of" or "characterized by"
- **Note**: Often creates positive quality descriptions

## Context
In morphology and word formation studies, -ful represents a productive suffix that creates adjectives expressing emotional or qualitative states.
