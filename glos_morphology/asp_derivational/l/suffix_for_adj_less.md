# -less

## Definition
The suffix **-less** is a derivational suffix in English that transforms adjectives into adjectives, meaning "without" or "lacking" the quality specified by the root.

## Details
- **Part of speech**: Suffix (derivational morpheme)
- **Function**: Negator - creates negative forms
- **Semantic effect**: Indicates absence or lack of a quality
- **Examples**:
  - **care** → **careless** (without care)
  - **hope** → **hopeless** (without hope)
  - **fear** → **fearless** (without fear)
  - **light** → **lightless** (without light)

## Pattern
- **Input**: Adjective or noun root
- **Output**: Adjective meaning "without"
- **Note**: Often creates negative opposites; may require vowel changes

## Context
In morphology and word formation studies, -less represents a productive negating pattern that creates adjectives expressing absence of qualities.
