# Adjective Suffixes in English

## Overview
English adjective suffixes are derivational morphemes that transform base words (typically nouns or adjectives) into adjective forms, expressing various semantic relationships like quality, state, manner, or possession.

## Classification

### Quality/State Suffixes
- **-ful** - full of, characterized by
- **-less** - without, lacking
- **-ous** - full of, characterized by (often emotional/qualitative)

### Manner Suffixes
- **-ly** - in a manner of, having the quality of

### Approximation/Similarity Suffixes
- **-ish** - like, somewhat, approximately
- **-like** - similar to, resembling

### Possession/Agency Suffixes
- **-er/-or** - one who performs (agent nouns)
- **-ive** - tending to, having the quality of

## Usage Patterns

| Suffix | Input | Output | Example |
|--------|-------|--------|---------|
| -ful | joy | joyful | full of joy |
| -less | care | careless | without care |
| -ous | danger | dangerous | full of danger |
| -ly | quick | quickly | in a quick manner |
| -ish | red | reddish | somewhat red |
| -ify | simple | simplify | make simple (verb) |
| -ize/-ise | modern | modernize | make modern (verb) |
| -ation | act | action | the act of acting (noun) |
